Guatemala City Central Park

The historic center of Guatemala City is the area occupied by the original settlement of Nueva Guatemala de la Asunción between 1776 and 1892, before the expansion of the city began towards the four cardinal points, especially towards the South. The historic center corresponds to the limits of the original layout of the city (today part of zone 1). The following historic buildings are located there: National Palace of Culture, Guatemala City Cathedral, Trade Portal, North Hippodrome and Relief Map, Post Office Building and Arch, Police Palace, Railway Museum, National Theater, Municipality of Guatemala, Parque Jocotenango (formerly called Parque Morazán), as well as areas such as the Barrio de la Candelaria and the Civic Center. It was declared "Cultural Heritage in the Guatemalan Nation", according to the agreement of the Ministry of Culture and Sports (MCD) 328-98 on August 13, 1998.
